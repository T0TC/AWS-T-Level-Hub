Students

T-Levels are a new kind of qualification from England that combine classroom learning with a substantial industry placement, giving students real workplace experience long before they graduate. For German students used to systems like the Abitur or vocational Ausbildung, T-Levels offer a similar blend of practical and academic learning, but with a direct route into UK industries such as digital technology, healthcare, construction, and engineering. This section helps students explore which T-Level pathway matches their interests, see what a typical placement looks like, and understand how the qualification could open doors to study or work opportunities in the UK.

Teachers & Schools

This section is designed for German schools and educators who may be advising students on international study options for the first time. It provides curriculum outlines, comparisons between T-Levels and familiar German qualifications, and practical guidance on how schools can support students interested in applying. Teachers will also find resources to help explain the placement structure to students and parents, along with information on how schools can establish a relationship as a recognized partner institution.

Parents & Guardians

Many German parents will not have encountered T-Levels before, so this section focuses on building trust and clarity around what the qualification involves, how it's recognized, and what kind of support structure exists around a placement abroad. It explains practical concerns such as safeguarding, supervision during industry placements, and how progress is communicated back to families, helping parents feel confident that their child is entering a well-structured and supported program rather than an unfamiliar gap-year alternative.

Accessibility

Since this portal serves an international audience navigating an unfamiliar education system, accessibility features are built in to remove language and usability barriers from the start. This includes a German-language toggle so families can read everything in their native language, adjustable font sizing for visual comfort, high-contrast display options, and screen-reader-friendly navigation. The goal is to make sure no student or parent is excluded from understanding their options simply because the system is new or the interface wasn't designed with them in mind.

Administration

This section is intended for Amazon staff responsible for running and maintaining the T-Level portal rather than for students, parents, or schools. From here, administrators can manage website content, updating course listings, placement opportunities, and translated text as the program grows. Administrators can also access submitted forms, reviewing applications, placement enquiries, and contact requests as they come in from students, parents, and partner schools. Finally, administrators can generate reports summarizing engagement, placement uptake, and course interest, giving the team visibility into how the program is performing across Germany and where outreach efforts may need to be adjusted.
